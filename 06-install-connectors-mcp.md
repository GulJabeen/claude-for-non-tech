## One thing to know first

You'll see two words used for this: **Connectors** and **MCP**.

They're the same thing. MCP is just the technical name. When you see it online — that's Connectors. Don't let it confuse you.

On its own, Claude can write, draft, plan, and design. But once you connect the tools you already use, Claude stops just talking about your work — it starts working *inside* it.

---

## How to install a Connector — 4 steps

1. Open **Claude Desktop**
2. Click **Settings → Connectors**
3. Search for the app you want (Gmail, Notion, Drive, Calendar...) and click **Connect**
4. Go back to chat and test it

No code. No setup. As easy as installing an app on your phone.

## The permissions tip nobody tells beginners

Every Connector shows you exactly what access it has — and you can switch each permission on or off.

Go to Settings → Connectors → click any connected app to see its permissions.

Browsing those permissions is the best way to get ideas for what to try next.

**Simplest test after connecting:** Ask Claude: *"Do you have access to my Notion?"* — it'll tell you instantly.

## Troubleshooting: 3 common issues + fixes

**Issue 1: "Claude doesn't seem to be using the Connector"**
Start a fresh chat. Connectors sometimes don't activate in an existing conversation. Be explicit: *"Use Notion to create a page called..."*

**Issue 2: "I connected it but Claude says it can't access my files"**
Go to Settings → Connectors, disconnect, and reconnect. Make sure you're signing in with the correct account.

**Issue 3: "The Connector installed but I can't find it"**
Close and reopen Claude Desktop. New Connectors sometimes need a restart. If it still doesn't show, check Settings → Connectors to confirm it's listed.
