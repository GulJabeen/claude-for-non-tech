## The three words everyone mixes up

Skill. Connector. Plugin. People throw these around and assume they mean the same thing. They don't. Here's the whole difference in one table — the easiest way to tell them apart is the **verb** you use.

| Term | The verb | What it actually is | Example |
|------|----------|---------------------|---------|
| **Skill** | you **write** it | A plain-English rulebook that tells Claude *how to sound* and *what rules to follow* | A "Brand Voice" Skill so every caption sounds like you |
| **Connector** | you **connect** it | A bridge to *your own* apps, accounts, and data | Gmail, Notion, Google Calendar, Slack |
| **Plugin** | you **install** it | A ready-made tool from Claude's library that gives it a *brand-new ability* | A topic-research tool or a design tool from claude.com/plugins |

---

## The 3-word memory hook

If you remember nothing else, remember this:

- **Skill = Instructions** (you tell Claude how to behave)
- **Connector = Access** (you give Claude reach into your tools)
- **Plugin = Superpower** (you give Claude a new ability)

Write a skill. Connect an app. Install a plugin.

---

## A workflow = the job that uses all three

A workflow is the actual task that strings them together. Miss one and it breaks. Here's the worked example from the episode:

```
1. PLUGIN     → finds a trending topic        (the new ability)
2. SKILL      → writes the post in your voice  (the rules / tone)
3. CONNECTOR  → saves it to your Notion        (where it lands)
```

Three different things. Working together. One finished post — done for you.

---

## Quick self-test

Ask yourself which verb fits, and you'll always know which one you need:

- "I want Claude to *sound like me*." → you need a **Skill** (write it)
- "I want Claude to *use my Gmail / Notion / Calendar*." → you need a **Connector** (connect it)
- "I want Claude to *do something new it can't do yet*." → you need a **Plugin** (install it)
