## The 3-line brief template

Before you type anything into Claude, fill in these three lines. Copy this, fill in the blanks, and paste it at the top of your message.

```
I need: [what you want Claude to do]
This is for: [who will read or use it]
Good looks like: [what a great result means to you]
```

---

## Example: business email

```
I need: a professional email declining a meeting request without offending the sender
This is for: a potential partner I want to keep a good relationship with
Good looks like: polite, brief (under 100 words), leaves the door open for future contact
```

Paste that into Claude, press Enter, and you'll get something usable immediately.

---

## The "push back" prompt

Paste this exact text when Claude jumps straight to an answer without asking you anything first. This forces it to slow down and check what you actually need.

```
Before you start, ask me anything you need to know to do this well.
```

You can use this at the start of any message, or add it after your brief.

---

## 3 common mistakes + fixes

**Mistake 1: Being too vague**
- Bad: "Write me an email"
- Fix: Tell Claude who it's for, what the goal is, and what tone you want

**Mistake 2: Asking for everything at once**
- Bad: "Write me a full marketing strategy, social posts, and email campaign"
- Fix: One task at a time. Finish the strategy first, then ask for posts.

**Mistake 3: Accepting the first draft**
- Bad: Copy-pasting the first thing Claude gives you
- Fix: Reply with "Make it shorter" / "Make it warmer" / "Try a different angle" — Claude improves fast when you push back
